# COURIER OPPORTUNITY INTELLIGENCE ARCHITECTURE

## THE FUNDAMENTAL SHIFT

Traditional lead enrichment asks: "Can we find an email address?"

Courier opportunity intelligence asks: "Does this business have a delivery problem that we solve?"

**Thesis**: Most courier CRMs track companies. Saint & Story should track delivery situations.

---

## CORE INSIGHT: DELIVERY SITUATIONS > COMPANY DATA

### Traditional Approach (Wrong)
```
Discover Company
  → Find contact email
  → Send generic pitch
  → Hope they need courier services
  → 2-5% conversion
```

### Intelligence-Driven Approach (Right)
```
Discover Company
  → Identify delivery situation
  → Calculate likelihood they need same-day courier
  → Calculate standing order potential
  → Calculate revenue potential
  → Rank by urgency
  → Call the 20 most likely prospects first
  → 15-30% conversion
```

## THE FOUR OPPORTUNITY SCORES

### 1. COURIER DEPENDENCY SCORE (0-100)
**Question**: How likely is this business to require same-day delivery?

**What it measures**: The inherent operational need for courier services in their business model.

**Examples**:
```
Estate Agent              = 95 (keys, contracts, completion docs)
Solicitor                = 90 (court documents, urgent filings)
Pharmacy                 = 85 (prescriptions, medical supplies)
Dentist/Dental Lab       = 85 (crowns, dentures, time-sensitive work)
Construction Supplier    = 80 (missing parts, project delay avoidance)
Medical Laboratory       = 85 (specimens, urgent results)
Accountant              = 65 (tax documents, filing deadlines)
Insurance Broker        = 60 (policies, urgent documents)
IT Hardware Supplier    = 70 (parts, remote office needs)
Architect               = 55 (drawings, urgent revisions)
Marketing Agency        = 30 (marketing materials, non-urgent)
Restaurant             = 20 (only for emergencies)
Retail Shop            = 25 (rare emergency restocks)
Hair Salon             = 10 (cosmetics non-urgent)
```

**Calculation**: Industry baseline × adjustment factors

**Adjustment Factors**:
- Multi-location: +20 pts (multiple sites = more transfers)
- "Urgent" in reviews: +15 pts
- "Same-day" mentioned: +20 pts
- "Emergency" mentioned: +25 pts
- "Delay" in negative reviews: +10 pts

**What it measures**: How often do they actually ship things (not just could)?

**Examples**:

```
Pharmacy                           = 85 (daily prescription deliveries)

Insurance Broker                   = 55 (sporadic urgent policies)

Manufacturing Company              = 85 (daily component shipments)
Solo Practitioner Solicitor        = 30 (sporadic, low volume)
Single-location Small Retail       = 15 (rare restocks)
Restaurant (small)                 = 5 (never regular couriers)
```

**Calculation**: Baseline frequency × volume indicators

**Volume Indicators**:
- Multi-office: +25 pts
- 10+ employees: +20 pts
- 20+ employees: +35 pts
- "Busy" in reviews: +10 pts
- "Fast-paced" in reviews: +10 pts
- Industry reputation for volume: +15 pts

**Question**: Do they deal with time-sensitive items where delays cost money?

**What it measures**: The financial/operational pain of delays.

**Examples**:
```
Court Document Deadline            = 100 (missed deadline = case lost)
Property Completion Closing         = 100 (fixed deadline = deal fails)
Medical Emergency Specimen          = 100 (time-sensitive test)
Construction Project (missing part) = 95 (crew sitting idle = $1000s/hr)
Insurance Policy Deadline           = 85 (policy cancellation risk)
Tax Filing Deadline                 = 80 (penalties + interest)
Manufacturing Shortage              = 90 (production line stops)
Pharmacy Prescription               = 75 (customer health impact)
Legal Appeal Deadline               = 95 (appeal window closes)
Immigration Filing Deadline         = 90 (visa expiration risk)
Event Supplies (day-of)            = 85 (event starts on schedule)
Marketing Campaign Launch           = 50 (delay = lost revenue opportunity)
Restaurant Delivery (food)         = 30 (food quality degrades)
Retail Restock                     = 20 (lost sales but not urgent)
```

**Calculation**: Base urgency × cost-of-delay multiplier

**Cost-of-Delay Factors**:
- Legal consequences: +25 pts
- Financial penalty: +20 pts
- Operational shutdown: +25 pts
- Health/safety impact: +20 pts
- Reputation damage: +15 pts
- Customer impact: +10 pts

### 4. REVENUE POTENTIAL (0-100)
**Question**: What's the estimated lifetime value if we win this account?

**What it measures**: Not company size. Courier usage volume.

**Examples**:
```
Large Law Firm (50+ lawyers)       = 95 (£5,000-15,000/mo estimated)
Estate Agent (major player)        = 90 (£3,000-10,000/mo estimated)
Pharmacy (high traffic)            = 85 (£2,000-8,000/mo estimated)
Hospital/Medical Lab               = 90 (£4,000-12,000/mo estimated)
Manufacturing Plant (100+ staff)   = 85 (£5,000-20,000/mo estimated)
Construction Firm                  = 80 (£3,000-8,000/mo estimated)
Insurance Broker                   = 60 (£1,000-3,000/mo estimated)
Accountant (20 staff)              = 65 (£800-2,000/mo estimated)
Solo Solicitor                     = 25 (£200-500/mo estimated)
Small Retail Shop                  = 15 (£100-300/mo estimated)
Restaurant                         = 10 (£50-200/mo estimated)
Hair Salon                         = 5 (£50-100/mo estimated)
```

**Calculation**: Base revenue estimate × usage frequency × competitive win likelihood

**Multipliers**:
- Industry average spend: 60%
- Usage frequency: 25%
- Switching likelihood: 15%

## MASTER SCORING FORMULA

```
OVERALL OPPORTUNITY SCORE = 
  (Courier Dependency × 0.25) +
  (Standing Order Potential × 0.30) +
  (Urgency Indicator × 0.25) +
  (Revenue Potential × 0.20)

Result: 0-100 score

Interpretation:
- 80-100: CALL FIRST (highest likelihood to buy)
- 60-79:  CALL SOON (strong prospect)
- 40-59:  CALL EVENTUALLY (medium prospect)
- 0-39:   LOW PRIORITY (rarely need courier)
```
