# Warm Outreach Sequence for Event Planning Companies
## Using Locked B2B Email Reasoning Engine

---

## EMAIL 1: Pattern-Based Observation
**Send: Day 1 (Tuesday-Thursday, 10 AM)**
**Goal: Establish relevance + curiosity**

```
Subject: Noticed your [Event Type] last month in [City]

Hi [First Name],

I came across your [specific event they ran] at [venue/date] — the setup looked intricate. 

Running coordinated events across multiple locations is harder than it looks. The logistics piece especially.

Thought you might find this interesting:
→ [Link to case study or 60-second video of a similar event coordination]

Happy to chat if you ever want to see how this could streamline your team's workflow.

[Your name]
Saint & Story | Logistics Coordination
```

**Reasoning (B2B Engine):**
- **Pattern observation:** Real observation (they ran an event you researched)
- **No ask:** Just insight sharing
- **Self-discovery:** They imagine how it applies to them
- **Micro-commitment:** Click the link or delete

## EMAIL 2: Pain Painting
**Send: Day 4 (same week, Thursday)**
**Goal: Surface specific pain + create "yes, that's us" moment**

```
Subject: What happened 2 hours before your events this month?

Hi [First Name],

Quick thought: I've watched 50+ event planners coordinate deliveries over the last 3 months.

Here's the pattern I see:
- 2 hours before setup: Courier says "running 30 mins late"
- You're panicking. Clients are calling. Staff is standing around.
- Caterer is now delayed. Linens still in van.
- You're out £500-1,000 in staff time you can't bill.

The courier? They don't care. You're one of 100 stops that day.

This is the cost nobody talks about.

Wondering if this sounds familiar, or if you've somehow solved this already.

[Your name]
Saint & Story | Logistics Coordination
```

**Reasoning (B2B Engine):**
- **Pain painting:** Specific sequence of what goes wrong
- **Quantification:** £500-1,000 cost (tangible loss)
- **Inverse incentive:** They're not just paying for a delivery—they're paying to avoid this panic
- **Question trigger:** Open-ended, invites mental response

## EMAIL 3: Inverse Incentive + Self-Discovery
**Send: Day 7 (next Monday)**
**Goal: Show what they GAIN by switching, not what they're missing**

```
Subject: What if they showed up exactly when you said?

Hi [First Name],

Here's a different angle:

Most event coordinators measure couriers by: "Do they show up?" (Low bar)

The good ones measure by: "Can I predict exactly when they show up?"

Prediction is worth more than cheap because:
- You can coordinate everything around it (caterers, staff, clients)
- Zero panic calls 2 hours before
- No standing-around staff time
- Your reputation never takes a hit

We work differently. Fixed time windows. One operator you know. Your phone gets answered.

Not cheaper. Better.

Worth a 10-minute call to see if this matters for your business?

[Your name]
Saint & Story | Logistics Coordination

P.S. — If your current setup already solves this, ignore this. But most planners I talk to say coordination (not cost) is the actual pain.
```

**Reasoning (B2B Engine):**
- **Inverse incentive:** "It's not about saving money, it's about keeping your reputation"
- **Self-discovery:** They realize coordination is their real problem
- **Specificity:** "Fixed time windows, one operator you know"
- **Micro-commitment:** 10-minute call (low friction ask)

```
Caterer needed delivery at 2:30 PM sharp (hot food). Client needed linens 20 mins before. Setup crew needed equipment at 2 PM.

Instead of 3 separate courier calls and panic, one window. One person. Everything arrived perfectly timed.

Event coordinator's note: "Finally, someone who actually thinks about the timing, not just the pickup."

That's the difference.

If you want to see what this looks like for YOUR events, I'm free for a quick call:
[Calendar link]

Otherwise, no worries. Just thought you should know this exists.

[Your name]
Saint & Story | Logistics Coordination
```

**Reasoning (B2B Engine):**
- **Social proof:** Real example (specific city, numbers, quote)
- **Specificity = credibility:** Shows understanding of their exact problem
- **Low friction call-to-action:** Calendar link (removes friction)
- **Graceful exit:** "Otherwise, no worries" (removes pressure)

## CRITICAL RULES (Locked B2B Engine)

✅ **DO:**
- Use specific, observed details (not generic "Dear Event Planner")
- Paint the pain with numbers/sequence, not platitudes
- Position on their gain (coordination), not your feature (cheap)
- Keep emails short (5-7 sentences max)
- Include one micro-commitment per email (link, call, reply)

❌ **DON'T:**
- Humanize after writing (no forced personality)
- Use "we're the best" or superlatives
- Mention price in first 3 emails (they don't care yet)
- Write about your company (only about their problem)
- Use generic subject lines

---

## PERSONALIZATION CHECKLIST

Before sending each email:
- [ ] Event they actually ran (find via Google, LinkedIn, Eventbrite, venue websites)
- [ ] Specific venue name or event type they handle
- [ ] Their first name (not "Hi there")
- [ ] Approximate event size or complexity if possible
- [ ] City/region where they operate
- [ ] Real detail in Email 4 example (use nearby city)

The observation must be **specific and verifiable** or it damages trust.
