# PROSPECT PAGE TRANSFORMATION FRAMEWORK

**How to apply PROSPECT_PAGE_DOCTRINE.md to all existing and future prospect pages.**

---

## The Transformation

### From Developer Thinking:
```
Section 1: Introduction
Section 2: Features
Section 3: Benefits
Section 4: Call-to-action
```

### To Salesperson Thinking:
```
Stage 1: Recognition (This is about them)
Stage 2: Understanding (We know their world)
Stage 3: Hidden Cost (They see the cost)
Stage 4: Transformation (They see a better future)
Stage 5: Identity Shift (They imagine who they become)
Stage 6: Action (One obvious next step)
```

---

## Step-by-Step Transformation

### Step 1: Understand the Prospect's World

**Question:** What does a business owner in this category actually experience every day?

**Not:** "What features can we sell them?"

**But:** "What problem keeps them up at night?"

For a solicitor: Missed court deadlines. Lost cases. Reputation damage.

For an estate agent: Failed key handovers. Lost transactions. Angry buyers.

For a medical practice: Delayed specimen collection. Concerned patients. Inefficiency.

**Output:** One specific, lived reality.

---

### Step 2: Write the Recognition Headline

**The headline must describe a reality, not a service.**

❌ **Wrong (Developer thinking):**
- "Professional Courier Services for Solicitors"
- "Reliable Delivery Solutions"
- "Same-Day Courier Service"

✅ **Right (Salesperson thinking):**
- "Court deadlines don't wait for anyone"
- "Every missed key handover is a lost deal"
- "Specimen delays cost patient care"

**Rule:** If the headline could be about any business in any industry, it's wrong.

---

### Step 3: Write Three Observations

Observations are not features.

They are patterns the prospect already lives with.

**Structure for each observation:**

1. **The Repetitive Challenge**
   - What happens regularly
   - What the prospect has learned to expect
   - Example: "Every deadline gets treated as equally urgent"

2. **The Hidden Cost**
   - What the repetition creates
   - What gets lost or damaged
   - Example: "Someone is always chasing delivery status"

3. **The Normalisation**
   - What the prospect has accepted as inevitable
   - What they've stopped questioning
   - Example: "You've accepted that some deadlines just won't be met"

**Example for Legal:**

Observation 1: "Court deadlines require certainty. But delivery timelines always remain uncertain."

Observation 2: "Someone is always chasing to confirm status. Some documents arrive in time. Some don't."

Observation 3: "You've learned to build in extra days. It works sometimes."

---

### Step 4: Write the Hidden Cost Section

This is where most pages fail.

This is where conversion happens.

**The hidden cost is not the problem. The hidden cost is what the problem creates.**

**Structure:**

1. Start with a fact about their world
2. Show the direct consequence
3. Show the secondary consequence (lost opportunities, damaged trust, etc.)
4. Show the normalisation (what they've stopped questioning)

**Example for Estate Agents:**

"Keys are always promised sooner than you can realistically deliver. Your team works to meet promises. Sometimes they do. Sometimes they don't.

When a key handover fails, the buyer already believes the transaction is in trouble. They start looking at other properties. Other agents. Other options.

You've learned not to promise specific times anymore. It's cost you deals."

**Rules:**

- No exaggeration
- No manufactured urgency
- No fear tactics
- Just clear, specific truth

---

### Step 5: Write the Transformation Section

This is about showing life after the problem disappears.

**Not service benefits. Transformation.**

**Structure:**

1. The new reality (what becomes possible)
2. What that enables (what changes as a result)
3. Who experiences that change (team, clients, business)

**Bad examples:**
- "Reliable same-day delivery"
- "Dedicated courier support"
- "Fast collections"

**Good examples:**
- "Your team stops chasing updates"
- "Deadlines stop feeling risky"
- "Clients stop asking where things are"

**Example for Estate Agents:**

"When every key is delivered exactly when promised, everything changes.

The buyer's confidence grows. Your team stops firefighting. Chains complete smoothly.

You operate like a business that knows how to execute."

**Rules:**

- Show the transformed state, not the service
- Concrete, not abstract
- Specific, not generic
- Credible, not fantasy
- Based on what you actually deliver

---

### Step 6: Write the Identity Shift Section

This is the deepest level.

People buy the person or company they want to become.

**Structure:**

1. What kind of business do you want to be known as?
2. What reputation do you want with clients?
3. What do your team believe about your reliability?

**Examples:**

**Solicitors:**
"Become the firm known for meeting every deadline."

**Estate Agents:**
"Become the agency where chains complete on schedule."

**Medical Practices:**
"Become the practice patients trust when timing matters."

**Manufacturing:**
"Become the supplier customers can depend on completely."

**Example for Estate Agents:**

"You become the agent who closes deals. The one clients trust. The one other agents watch. The one who delivers."

**Rules:**

- Focus on identity, not service
- Show what reputation is possible
- Connect to their ambition
- Make it personal

---

### Step 7: Write the One CTA

**There is only one action.**

**Structure:**

1. Outcome-driven verb (not "contact us")
2. What you're inviting them to do
3. No alternative actions

**Examples:**

- "Let's see what's being missed" (instead of "contact us")
- "Talk through this together" (instead of "book a call")
- "Show me how this works" (instead of "learn more")
- "Stop another deadline from slipping" (instead of "get started")

**Rule:** The CTA invites conversation, not commitment.

## The Transformation Checklist

### Recognition Stage
- [ ] Headline describes a reality they live with (not a service)
- [ ] Within 5 seconds, the prospect thinks "that's us"
- [ ] No generic language
- [ ] Specific to their category
- [ ] At least 3 moments of recognition throughout

### Understanding Stage
- [ ] Three observations (not features, not benefits)
- [ ] Each observation triggers recognition
- [ ] Specific to their daily experience
- [ ] No sales language
- [ ] Prospect nods while reading
- [ ] Observation-based, not claim-based

### Hidden Cost Stage
- [ ] Reveals specific consequences (not just the problem)
- [ ] Shows what the problem actually costs them
- [ ] No exaggeration or fear tactics
- [ ] Visitor feels clarity, not manipulation
- [ ] Clear connection to their world
- [ ] Truth-based, not manufactured

### Transformation Stage
- [ ] Shows life after the problem (not service benefits)
- [ ] Concrete and specific about new reality
- [ ] Credible based on actual capabilities
- [ ] Visitor sees what becomes possible
- [ ] Not fantasy or hype
- [ ] Outcome-focused language

### Identity Shift Stage
- [ ] Helps prospect imagine who they become
- [ ] Specific to their business type
- [ ] Focuses on reputation and identity
- [ ] Personal and aspirational
- [ ] Connected to their ambition
- [ ] Shows a better version of themselves

### Action Stage
- [ ] One CTA only
- [ ] Outcome-driven wording
- [ ] Invites conversation, not commitment
- [ ] No competing buttons
- [ ] Obvious next step
- [ ] Visitor feels "this is what I should do"

### Overall
- [ ] Visually matches homepage atmosphere
- [ ] Human language only (no AI/marketing speak)
- [ ] Less is more philosophy
- [ ] No unnecessary elements
- [ ] Every section earns its place
- [ ] Six-stage psychological journey intact

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Feature-Based Copy
```
"We offer same-day delivery with real-time tracking"
```

### ✅ Problem-Based Copy
```
"When your deadline lands, your reputation grows"
```

---

### ❌ Multiple CTAs
```
"Contact Us" | "Book a Call" | "Learn More"
```

### ✅ Single CTA
```
"Let's see what's being missed"
```

---

### ❌ Generic Language
```
"Leading courier solutions for modern businesses"
```

### ✅ Specific Language
```
"Your court deadlines can't wait. Neither can we."
```

---

### ❌ No Hidden Cost Section
Just features → benefits → call to action

### ✅ Full Psychological Flow
Recognition → Understanding → Tension → Relief → Action

---

### ❌ Developer/Designer Thinking
"What's the optimal information architecture?"
"How do we present all the features?"
"What sections should we include?"

### ✅ Salesperson Thinking
"What does this person already live with?"
"What cost do they not see clearly?"
"What one action would move them forward?"

## Implementation Path

### For Existing Pages (12 pages)

1. **Identify the prospect category**
   - Legal
   - Estate Agent
   - Medical
   - Construction
   - Accounting
   - Insurance
   - Other

2. **Apply transformation steps 1-6 in order**
   - Don't skip recognition (most critical)
   - Don't skip tension (most impactful)
   - Don't skip relief (most credible)

3. **Test against checklist**
   - Every box must be checked
   - If any box is empty, the page needs revision

4. **Visual check**
   - Does it feel like the homepage?
   - Does it feel expensive?
   - Is it obviously not a template?

### For New Pages

Every new prospect page MUST follow this framework.

No exceptions.

---

## The Difference It Makes

### Before Transformation (Developer Thinking):
- Visitor: "Here's information about their service"
- Visitor feels: "This is interesting but not about me"
- Action rate: Low (why should I act?)

### After Transformation (Salesperson Thinking):
- Visitor Stage 1: "That's exactly us"
- Visitor Stage 2: "They understand my world"
- Visitor Stage 3: "This is costing us more than we thought"
- Visitor Stage 4: "I can see a better way"
- Visitor Stage 5: "I want to become that kind of business"
- Visitor Stage 6: "I know exactly what to do"
- Action rate: High (action becomes obvious)

That's the salesperson difference.
