## WHAT WAS BUILT

### Layer 1: Epistemic Framework
Tracks evidence type at every reasoning step:
- Observation (direct data)
- Inference (logical conclusions)
- Hypothesis (educated guesses)
- Assumption (taken as true)
- Speculation (insufficient evidence)

**Purpose:** Never confuse observation with hypothesis. Always know what level of certainty each claim has.

**Example:** 4.8★ vs 3.2★ (observation, 95%) → variance exists (inference, 85%) → owner managing personally (hypothesis, 75%)

---

### Layer 2: Confidence Calibration
Maps evidence quality to honest confidence scores:
- Evidence quality scoring
- Recency penalties (data degrades with age)
- Contradiction penalties (conflicts reduce confidence)
- Graceful degradation (confidence → language mapping)

**Purpose:** 80% confidence should actually mean 80% accuracy, not arbitrary numbers.

**Example:**
- 85%+ confidence → "You are managing..."
- 70-85% confidence → "You appear to be managing..."
- 55-70% confidence → "You may be managing..."

---

### Layer 3: Multi-Hypothesis Engine
Before committing to one explanation:
- Generate all plausible hypotheses
- Evaluate each against evidence
- Select primary with confidence
- Retain alternatives
- Express uncertainty if runner-up is close

**Purpose:** Prevent false certainty from premature commitment.

**Example:**
- Primary: Quality Management (75%)
- Runner-up: Hiring Gap (70%)
- Gap: 5% (too close → acknowledge uncertainty)

---

### Layer 4: Outcome Learning
Close the loop with evidence from real outcomes:
- Record post-send signals
- Capture operator/prospect feedback
- Calculate Recognition Accuracy (north-star metric)
- Validate confidence calibration
- Generate learning updates

**Purpose:** System improves from what actually works.

**Example:**
- 80% of prospects said recognition was accurate
- Quality type: 67% accuracy, Capacity type: 100%
- Predicted confidence (70%) vs actual (80%) → needs recalibration
