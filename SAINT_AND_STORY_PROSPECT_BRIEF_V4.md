## CORE PHILOSOPHY

The prospect brief is not a landing page.

It is not a report.

It is not a brochure.

**It is a prospect identification and conversion system.**

The objective is to identify the decision maker inside the target company and begin a sales conversation.

The page should feel like:

"We understand your world."

Never:

"We are trying to sell you something."

---

## PRIMARY OBJECTIVE

Every prospect page should produce:

* Name
* Role
* Company
* Contact details

from the person experiencing the problem.

The prospect should leave feeling:

**"This company understands our operational reality."**

before any sales conversation takes place.

---

## CORE POSITIONING

Every prospect page must make one thing obvious:

**Saint & Story is a same-day courier company.**

We are not consultants.

We are not marketers.

We are not software.

We solve urgent delivery, collection and deadline problems.

The page should never leave visitors wondering:

"What does this company actually do?"

The service must be clear within seconds.

---

## HERO SECTION RULES

### Company Name Display (REQUIRED)

**REMOVE ALL INDUSTRY LABELS.**

Never display: Legal, Solicitors, Estate Agents, Healthcare, Construction, Architecture, Accounting, etc.

The position currently occupied by category labels must display:

**THE COMPANY NAME**

Example:

```
NATIONAL LEGAL SERVICE SOLICITORS
```

This creates immediate recognition.

The visitor should instantly understand:

"This page was prepared specifically for us."

### Visual Recognition System (REQUIRED)

Every prospect page must include a category-specific visual.

The visual should instantly reflect the prospect's world.

Purpose: Visitor recognises their world before reading copy.

**Visual Examples:**

| Category | Visual |
|----------|--------|
| Legal | Document icon or briefcase visual |
| Estate Agents | Keys or property visual |
| Construction | Materials or building site visual |
| Healthcare | Prescription or medical visual |
| Accounting | Files or financial visual |
| Architecture | Plans or blueprint visual |

The visual is a category-specific indicator.

It is not branding.

It is recognition.

## CTA PHILOSOPHY

### Universal CTA (REQUIRED)

The page does not ask for a sale.

The page does not ask for commitment.

The page does not ask for a quote.

**The page asks for a conversation.**

### CTA Text (STANDARD)

```
START THE CONVERSATION
```

This CTA is now standard across all industries.

**No variations.**

**No alternatives.**

**No category-specific buttons.**

The personalization happens inside the page.

The CTA reduces friction.

---

## EMAIL CONVERSION SYSTEM (REQUIRED)

The CTA opens a pre-populated email.

The visitor should never face a blank email.

The page should do the thinking.

The visitor should only identify themselves.

### Email Structure

**To:** james@saintandstory.co.uk

**Subject:**

```
[COMPANY NAME] — Urgent Collections & Deliveries
```

**Body (Pre-populated):**

```
Hello James,

I came across the page you prepared for us.

A few of the points you highlighted felt familiar.

I'd like to understand how Saint & Story could help us improve our urgent deliveries and collections.

Name:
Role:
Company:
Best contact number:

Kind regards,
```

The prospect only fills in the blanks.

## TRANSFORMATION PRINCIPLE

### Saint & Story Does Not Sell Transport

Saint & Story sells transformation.

Every prospect page must clearly show:

**Current State → Future State**

#### Examples

**Legal:**
- Current: Your team spends time chasing deliveries
- Future: Your team trusts the delivery process and focuses on clients

**Estate Agents:**
- Current: Deadlines feel fragile
- Future: Deadlines become routine

**Healthcare:**
- Current: Urgent collections create stress
- Future: Collections happen without thought

**Construction:**
- Current: Site delays compound costs
- Future: Projects stay on schedule

The prospect must see themselves moving from one state to another.

**Transformation is the product.**

**Transport is the mechanism.**

---

## COST SECTION (REQUIRED)

Every page must contain a cost section.

The visitor must understand:

**What happens if nothing changes.**

Not through fear.

Through reality.

The page should reveal:

* Time lost
* Opportunities missed
* Reputation damage
* Operational drag
* Team frustration

The cost must feel believable.

Never exaggerated.
