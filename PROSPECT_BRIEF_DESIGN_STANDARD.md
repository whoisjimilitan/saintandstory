# SAINT & STORY PROSPECT BRIEF SYSTEM

## Permanent Design and Conversion Standard

**THIS DOCUMENT IS NON-NEGOTIABLE**

All prospect brief design, copy, and interaction decisions must follow these principles without exception.

---

## Core Principle

A prospect brief is not a report.

A prospect brief is not a dashboard.

A prospect brief is not a research page.

**A prospect brief is a highly personalised sales asset designed to move a prospect toward one action.**

Every design decision must serve that objective.

The purpose of the page is simple:

**The prospect should feel: "This company understands my problem better than I do."**

Once that happens, resistance falls dramatically.

---

# Philosophy

The Saint & Story prospect page must feel like the homepage.

Not merely use the same colours.

Not merely use the same typography.

**It must carry the same atmosphere.**

The homepage philosophy is:

* less is more
* calm confidence
* premium restraint
* thoughtful spacing
* human language
* no clutter
* no corporate noise
* no aggressive sales tactics
* no visual chaos

The prospect page should feel as though it was handcrafted for one company.

Because psychologically, it was.

---

# The Job Of The Page

The page exists to accomplish five psychological stages.

## Stage 1: Recognition

The prospect immediately feels:

**"This is about us."**

Not: "This is about businesses like us."

Not: "This could apply to us."

Specifically: **"This is about us."**

Recognition must happen within five seconds.

---

## Stage 2: Understanding

The prospect must believe:

**"You understand our world."**

Not: "You understand marketing."

Not: "You understand websites."

Not: "You understand logistics."

**Their world.**

Their frustrations.

Their risks.

Their pressures.

Their customers.

Their reputation.

Their deadlines.

Their lost opportunities.

---

## Stage 3: Tension

The page must expose a cost.

Not through fear.

Not through manipulation.

**Through clarity.**

The prospect should realise:

* "We are leaking opportunities."
* "We have accepted problems that shouldn't exist."
* "We are leaving money on the table."
* "We are losing customers without realising it."

**The prospect should arrive at that conclusion themselves.**

Never force it.

---

## Stage 4: Certainty

Once tension exists, the page provides clarity.

Not twenty possible solutions.

Not multiple directions.

**One.**

The prospect should feel:

**"There is a practical next step."**

Not: "There is a lot to think about."

Thinking reduces conversion.

Clarity increases conversion.

---

## Stage 5: Action

**One action.**

Only one.

Never multiple CTAs.

Never competing actions.

Never:
* Book a call
* Email us
* Learn more
* Download
* Contact us
* Schedule

**Choose one.**

Everything points toward it.

---

# Prospect Psychology Framework

Every prospect page should answer these silent questions:

**Question 1:** Do you understand us?

**Question 2:** Do you understand our problem?

**Question 3:** Can you help?

**Question 4:** Can I trust you?

**Question 5:** What should I do next?

**Every section exists to answer one of those questions.**

Nothing else.

---

# Language Standards

### Absolutely no AI language.

Never use:
* leverage
* optimise
* unlock
* maximise
* transformative
* game changing
* cutting edge
* seamless
* robust
* innovative
* industry leading

Never sound like software.

Never sound like consultants.

Never sound like LinkedIn.

Never sound like ChatGPT.

**Language must sound like a thoughtful human speaking directly to another human.**

---

# Writing Style

**Short.**

**Precise.**

**Clear.**

**Human.**

Every sentence must earn its place.

If a sentence can be removed without harming conversion, remove it.

If a paragraph can become one sentence, make it one sentence.

If a section can become three lines, make it three lines.

---

# Design Standards

The homepage is the reference.

**Always.**

Prospect pages inherit:
* typography
* spacing
* rhythm
* layout discipline
* premium restraint
* visual hierarchy

Never introduce:
* dashboards
* tables
* cards everywhere
* excessive borders
* visual clutter
* enterprise software aesthetics

**The page should feel expensive.**

Not busy.

---

# Page Structure

## Section 1: Personalised Hero

Directly reference the company.

Show understanding.

**Example structure:**

Company Name

A short observation that demonstrates understanding of their world.

One sentence.

Not a paragraph.

---

## Section 2: What We Noticed

Three observations.

Not generic.

Specific.

Human.

Short.

Each should trigger recognition.

The prospect should nod.

Not read.

---

## Section 3: The Hidden Cost

**This is the emotional centre.**

Show what these issues create:

* Lost time
* Lost trust
* Lost opportunities
* Lost revenue
* Lost referrals
* Lost repeat business

Not dramatic.

Accurate.

---

## Section 4: The Opportunity

Simple.

Practical.

Concrete.

Show the better future.

Not fantasy.

Not hype.

Just clarity.

---

## Section 5: Single Action

One button.

One outcome.

One instruction.

No alternatives.

No competing paths.

---

# CTA Rules

**Every page gets one CTA.**

Only one.

The CTA should feel like relief.

Not commitment.

**Examples:**

* Let's look at this together
* Show me what's being missed
* Let's fix this
* Can we show you something?

**The CTA must match the identified pain.**

---

# Conversion Rule

If a prospect skims the page for thirty seconds they should still understand:

1. You understand them.
2. There is a problem.
3. The problem is costing them.
4. There is a practical solution.
5. There is one next step.

**If those five outcomes are not achieved, the page fails.**

---

# Final Test

Before any prospect page is published ask:

* Would this page feel believable if the business owner read it?
* Would they feel understood?
* Would they feel something important was noticed?
* Would they know exactly what to do next?

If not, rebuild it.

**Never ship informational pages.**

**Only ship conversion assets.**
