## Hour 1: Target Selection & List Building

**What:** Pick your first customer segment and build a list of 100 businesses.

**Why this hour:** You need targets before you can call. Choose strategically.

### Step 1: Pick Your First Segment (15 minutes)

**Don't target:** "All businesses"

**Target businesses where:**
- Deliveries happen regularly
- Time matters (urgency creates opportunity)
- Mistakes are expensive (high-value problem)
- The owner/manager is reachable

**Best starting targets:**

**Professional Offices**
- Solicitors
- Accountants
- Estate agents
- Insurance brokers
- Recruitment agencies
- Why? They send documents, contracts, files. Urgency matters.

**Property Businesses**
- Estate agents
- Property managers
- Landlords
- Why? Keys, inspections, documents. Time-sensitive.

**Medical Businesses**
- Clinics
- Pharmacies
- Labs
- Why? Urgency and reliability are non-negotiable.

**Pick ONE segment to start with.** You'll expand to others after Day 1.

### Step 2: Build Your List (45 minutes)

**Tools:** Google Maps, LinkedIn, Local business directories, Yellow Pages, Trustpilot

**Create a spreadsheet with these columns:**

```
Business | Contact Name | Phone | Email | Notes | Status
```

**Example:**

```
ABC Solicitors | Jane Smith | 020 7XXX XXXX | jane@abc-sol.co.uk | 5 people, city center | Not contacted
```

**Target:** 100 businesses by end of hour 1.

**Don't overthink:**
- Use Google Maps search: "Solicitors near me" or "[Your City] solicitors"
- Click each result, note the phone number
- Search LinkedIn: "[City] solicitor" and note their contact details
- Use local business directories

**Quality over perfection:** You want contact info and basic context. That's it.

## Hours 2-5: Make Calls (240 minutes)

**What:** Call 50 businesses. Have real conversations with 15+.

**Why this hour:** This is where the strategy becomes real.

### The Phone Script (Not a pitch—a conversation)

**Opening (First 10 seconds):**

```
"Hi [Name], I'm [Your Name] from Saint & Story. 
We help local businesses with reliable deliveries and removals. 
I'm calling because I wanted to ask a quick question—
how do you currently handle urgent documents, keys, or items that need moving?"
```

**Then STOP and listen.**

### What You'll Hear (And How to Respond)

**Response 1: "We already have a courier"**

✅ Good answer. They use couriers.

**Your follow-up:**
```
"That's good. Are they reliable enough that you never need a backup? 
Or do situations come up where you need someone to call in an emergency?"
```

**Why:** You're not replacing their current courier. You're becoming the backup.

---

**Response 2: "We don't really need deliveries"**

✅ Also good. Opens the conversation.

**Your follow-up:**
```
"Fair enough. Out of curiosity, what's one operational thing that wastes your team's time? 
What's the biggest pain point in how you run things day-to-day?"
```

**Why:** You're building intelligence about their business. You're looking for any problem you might solve (now or later).

---

**Response 3: "Actually, yes, we do have issues"**

✅ JACKPOT.

**Your follow-up:**
```
"Tell me more. What's the specific situation? 
When does this usually happen? 
How are you solving it now?"
```

Then listen. Take notes. At the end:

```
"Let me look into this and come back to you with an option. 
When's a good time to call you back this week?"
```

### Call Flow (5-7 minutes per call)

1. **Opening** (10 sec) — State who you are, why you're calling
2. **Question** (5 sec) — Ask how they handle urgent movement/deliveries
3. **Listen** (2 min) — Let them talk
4. **Follow-up** (2 min) — Ask clarifying questions based on their answer
5. **Close** (1 min) — "I'll look into this and call you back"

**Total:** 5-7 minutes per call. Most will be shorter.

### Your Daily Targets

**Hour 2:** 10 calls → aim for 3 conversations  
**Hour 3:** 10 calls → aim for 3 conversations  
**Hour 4:** 10 calls → aim for 3 conversations  
**Hour 5:** 10 calls → aim for 3 conversations

### The Key: Listen More Than You Talk

You're not selling. You're understanding.

The best calls are when they do 70% of the talking and you do 30%.

## Hours 5-7: Follow Up With Warm Leads (120 minutes)

**What:** Visit or call the 3-5 warmest prospects.

**Why now:** Strike while the conversation is fresh.

### The Follow-Up (Same Day)

**By phone or in person:**

```
"Hi [Name], I wanted to follow up on our call earlier about [specific problem]. 
I've thought about how we could help.

Here's what I'm thinking: [Simple solution]

When could we make this happen?"
```

**Then present the first offer (see below).**

### The First Offer (Make It Easy)

**Don't sell a big contract.**

**Offer this:**

```
"Let us be your reliable backup courier. No commitment. 
If something urgent comes up, you have someone you can call.
Your first job is [date/time]. Let's see how it goes."
```

**Why:** Zero friction. They're not buying a service. They're trying you out once.

**Price:** Your standard rate. Don't discount. Premium doesn't discount.

### The Close

```
"When do you need something moved/delivered? 
Let's book your first job."
```

## The Operating Questions (Ask Every Business)

Before you hang up, ask:

```
"By the way, is there any other operational problem you're trying to solve right now?"
```

This does two things:
1. Reveals more business problems (Layer 2 intelligence)
2. Shows you're interested in helping, not just selling
