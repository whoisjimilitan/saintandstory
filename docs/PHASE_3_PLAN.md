# PHASE 3 PLAN: Reality Capture

**Status**: Planning only (no implementation)
**Purpose**: Expose system to reality before building intelligence
**Duration**: 8-12 weeks
**Goal**: 100 real conversations with business owners

---

## CORE PRINCIPLE

**Reality must arrive before intelligence.**

Until we have meaningful real-world conversation data:
- We are building a laboratory, not a learning system
- We cannot measure hypothesis accuracy
- We cannot optimize anything
- We cannot rank anything
- We cannot predict anything

Phase 3 is not about making the system smarter.

Phase 3 is about exposing the system to reality.

---

## THE WORKFLOW

### 1. OUTREACH WORKFLOW

**Who reaches out**: James (human)

**How**:
- Phone call only (no email, no automation)
- Target: High-potential businesses (from Phase 2 ranking)
- Timing: One call per business per week
- Goal: Get business owner on phone

**Script Framework**:
```
"Hi [Owner], I'm James from Saint & Story. 
We work with [industry] businesses.
I came across your [business name] and saw you handle a lot of 
[wedding work / seasonal demand / custom orders].
I had one quick question - do you have 5 minutes?"
```

**Expected outcomes**:
- Owner agrees to talk (target: 50%)
- Owner says "not interested" (target: 40%)
- Owner says "not now" (target: 10%)

---

### 2. CONVERSATION WORKFLOW

**Setting**: Phone call (5-15 minutes)

**Structure** (EXACTLY THIS):

**Step 1: Opening Question** (generated by Phase 2.5 system)
- One question based on review analysis
- Example: "You handle a lot of wedding work. How many components does a typical wedding order have?"
- DO NOT: Pitch, explain system, discuss standing orders yet

**Step 2: Listen** (6-10 minutes)
- Owner talks
- James takes NOTES (not transcription)
- Follow-up only if owner asks question

**Step 3: Close** (optional)
- If conversation is productive: "This is helpful. Can I follow up in a few weeks?"
- If conversation is dead: "Thanks for your time."
- NO offer yet. NO proposal. NO pitch.

**What success looks like**:
- Owner answered substantively
- Owner engaged beyond yes/no
- Owner revealed operational reality
- James learned something unexpected

**What failure looks like**:
- Owner gave one-word answers
- Owner seemed annoyed
- James couldn't understand their business
- No new information emerged

---

### 3. LOGGING WORKFLOW

**Immediately after call** (while details are fresh):

**Location**: `/app/conversation/[id]` (UI already built)

**Log EXACTLY**:

```
conversationId: [auto-generated]
businessId: [from Phase 2]
question: [question asked]

outcome: {
  signalType: "contacted" (they answered the phone)
  truthLevel: "direct" (they spoke to us)
  signalClassification: one of:
    - "supports_hypothesis" (they confirmed what reviews suggested)
    - "partially_supports" (some parts confirmed)
    - "contradicts_hypothesis" (reviews were misleading)
    - "irrelevant_signal" (we learned something else)
    - "unknown" (not sure what we learned)
  notes: [raw observations]
}
```

**Notes format** (NOT structured):
```
- Owner said X
- When asked Y, they responded Z
- Repeated emphasis on [topic]
- Seemed stressed about [thing]
- Mentioned [unexpected detail]
- Solution they are considering: [info]
- Budget perception: [info]
- Team size: [info]
- Current tools/systems: [info]
```

**DO NOT**:
- Score their potential
- Rank against other businesses
- Predict conversion
- Estimate deal size
- Judge their business
- Form conclusions

**DO**:
- Record exactly what was said
- Note tone/emotion (seemed stressed, upbeat, dismissive)
- Record unexpected information
- Note what contradicts reviews

### 4. WHAT CONSTITUTES USEFUL LEARNING

Data is useful when it:

**✅ Validates a hypothesis**:
```
Hypothesis: "Owner is personally involved in operations"
Evidence: Owner said "I personally design every wedding"
Status: CONFIRMED
```

**✅ Contradicts a hypothesis**:
```
Hypothesis: "Manual coordination is a bottleneck"
Evidence: Owner said "We've automated 80% of coordination"
Status: CONTRADICTED
```

**✅ Reveals operational reality**:
```
Unexpected: Owner said "Our biggest problem is actually staffing retention, not workload"
Learning: Current framework doesn't detect staffing pressure
Action: Add new hypothesis for Phase 2 detection
```

**✅ Reveals business priorities**:
```
Owner priorities (in order):
1. Consistent quality
2. Staff happiness (retention)
3. Revenue growth
Action: Standing orders may not address actual pain
```

**✅ Shows market willingness**:
```
Owner said: "If we had reliable delivery service, we'd use it"
Evidence: Unprompted mention of standing orders concept
Status: Market signal exists
```

---

### 5. WHAT CONSTITUTES FAILURE

Outcomes that tell us we're on wrong path:

**❌ Hypothesis accuracy < 30%**:
If hypotheses don't match reality in > 70% of conversations:
- Phase 2 framework is flawed
- Review analysis misses real signals
- Different niche? Different pain points?

**❌ Conversation engagement < 40%**:
If < 4 of 10 owners engage substantively:
- Question quality is wrong
- Outreach approach is wrong
- Business segment is wrong

**❌ Contradictions accumulate**:
If same hypothesis is contradicted in 5+ conversations:
- Hypothesis is false
- Remove from Phase 2 framework

**❌ No new information after 25 conversations**:
If data starts repeating with no new insights:
- Sample exhausted
- Need different business segment
- Insights are complete

**❌ Owner disinterest in standing orders**:
If no owner mentions interest (unprompted) after 30 conversations:
- Core market assumption is wrong
- Product-market fit doesn't exist
- Pivot needed

## EVIDENCE THAT JUSTIFIES LEARNING-SYSTEM WORK

A learning system (scoring, ranking, prediction) is justified when:

**✅ Clear pattern exists**:
```
"Businesses with X characteristic convert at 60%+
Businesses without X convert at 10%-"
Pattern is based on 50+ real conversations
Pattern is reproducible across conversations
```

**✅ Distinguishing signal is identifiable**:
```
"Owners who mention 'standing orders' unprompted
convert at 5x higher rate than those who don't"
This is measurable from real conversations
Not speculated from reviews
```

**✅ Enough data to build on**:
```
Minimum 25 positive outcomes (interested owners)
Minimum 25 negative outcomes (uninterested owners)
To build prediction model that predicts either
```

**✅ Business case is clear**:
```
"If we contacted 100 owners,
~30 would be interested,
At $X per contract, that's $Y revenue"
This is based on real conversation data
Not on hypothetical modeling
```

## EVIDENCE THAT PROVES ASSUMPTIONS WRONG

Current assumptions we expect to test:

**Assumption 1: Owner involvement predicts opportunity**
```
Test: Do owners mentioned by name in reviews actually 
      express interest in standing orders?

Falsifies if: <20% of "owner-involved" businesses 
             show interest after conversation
```

**Assumption 2: Wedding work creates pressure**
```
Test: Do businesses doing wedding work express 
     pain/pressure in conversation?

Falsifies if: Wedding businesses mention no operational stress
             or say standing orders wouldn't help
```

**Assumption 3: Seasonal peaks create demand**
```
Test: Do owners managing seasonal peaks express 
     interest in smoothing demand?

Falsifies if: Owners say seasonal peaks aren't a problem
             or they prefer unpredictable work
```

**Assumption 4: Manual coordination is bottleneck**
```
Test: Do owners doing manual coordination express 
     desire for automation?

Falsifies if: Owners prefer manual coordination
             or have already automated it
             or don't see it as limiting
```

**Assumption 5: Standing orders are valuable**
```
Test: Do business owners express interest in 
     recurring delivery contracts?

Falsifies if: <10% of owners mention interest
             or all express skepticism about recurring model
             or their business model incompatible with it
```

**Assumption 6: Manchester florists are representative**
```
Test: Do insights from Manchester florists apply to:
      - Other UK cities
      - Other industries (restaurants, retailers)

Falsifies if: Patterns are completely different
             in different geographies / industries
```
