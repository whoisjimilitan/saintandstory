### 1. DISCOVERY (Entry Point)
```
Prospect enters pipeline (Google Places, file upload, inbound, or referral)
    ↓
Auto-enrichment: Observations, pain points, business patterns captured
    ↓
Pressure type identified: Which operational burden do they face?
    ↓
Psychology engine has complete context
```

### 2. WAVE 1: Recognition Email
```
EMAIL RECEIVED:

"One thing that stood out about haart: Your best branch gets 4.8★ reviews. 
Your newer branch gets 3.2★. Clients consistently mention the difference.

This probably sounds familiar: you're spending time managing inconsistent 
service quality across branches when you should be focusing on growth.

We've built our process around removing that burden. Last week alone, 
we handled 12 similar situations for estate-agents businesses.

Quick question: Some clients get great service, others see cracks—does 
that match your experience?"

GATE 1: Email delivered ✅ (contact valid)
GATE 2 WAIT: 72 hours
    ↓ (If opened) → CONTINUE
    ↓ (If not opened) → FOLLOW-UP 1 (different pressure angle)
```

### 3. WAVE 2: Trust Page
```
GATE 2 PASSED: Email opened ✅ (prospect is curious)

PROSPECT VISITS BRIEF PAGE:

(Page continues email narrative, doesn't repeat it)

Acknowledgment: Shows they heard burden
Proof: Case study of multi-branch solution
Process: Explains HOW consistency is ensured
Their Reality: "For your 12 branches specifically, here's how it works"
Urgency: Cost of NOT solving
CTA: Validation question (not sales close)

GATE 3 WAIT: 24 hours
    ↓ (If visited) → CONTINUE
    ↓ (If not visited) → FOLLOW-UP 2 (scarcity + urgency)
```

### 4. WAVE 2.5: Engagement & Response

**GATE 3 PASSED: Page visited ✅ (prospect is considering)**

#### Prospect Replies
```
PROSPECT SENDS: "How does this work for our 12 branches?"

SYSTEM RESPONSE:
1. Post-engagement router receives reply
2. Psychology engine analyzes reply:
   - Intent: Wants to understand methodology
   - Stage: Engaged → Trust building
   - Hidden objection: "Will this work at OUR scale?"
3. Psychology engine generates OPERATOR CONTEXT BRIEF:
   - What to say (structure, not template)
   - What NOT to say (guardrails)
   - How to reference their specific question
   - How to show methodology (not claims)
   - How to make it concrete for THEIR 12 branches
4. Brief goes to operator dashboard
```

#### Operator Responds
```
OPERATOR READS BRIEF:

"PROSPECT: haart
THEIR QUESTION: How does this work for our 12 branches?
YOUR JOB: Build trust by showing HOW you ensure consistency at scale

STRUCTURE:
1. Start: Reference their specific 12-branch question
2. Acknowledge: Show you understand scale challenge
3. Explain: Methodology (not claims, actual process)
4. Proof: Case study of similar scale
5. Their reality: How it works for THEIR 12 branches
6. Validation: Not a close, a question"

OPERATOR WRITES:
"You asked how this works for 12 branches—perfect question.

At 12 locations, inconsistency compounds exponentially. That's what you're 
personally managing.

Here's the methodology: Rather than you managing consistency manually, 
the process surfaces issues automatically. When variance appears, it's visible 
immediately—not hidden in late client complaints. Your managers don't police 
each other; the system shows what's different and why.

We did this with Westfield Properties (9 locations). Their variance dropped 80% 
in 60 days. More importantly, managers stopped spending time on consistency 
politics and started managing clients.

For your 12 branches: Your strongest branches (4.8★) become the standard. 
The system ensures that standard is consistent across all 12.

Does that approach match what you were looking for?"

QUALITY GATE: Wave 4 (Human Writing Engine) validates before sending
    ✅ Specific observation → PASS
    ✅ Methodology explained → PASS
    ✅ Proof provided → PASS
    ✅ Their reality referenced → PASS
    ✅ No AI clichés → PASS
    
EMAIL SENT ✅

GATE 4: Reply received ✅ (prospect is engaged)
GATE 5 WAIT: 48 hours
    ↓ (If conversation continues) → GATE 5 PASSED
    ↓ (If silence) → FOLLOW-UP 3 (operator calls)
```

## Follow-Up Sequences (Each Uses Different Angle)

### Follow-Up 1 (If Gate 2 fails: Email not opened after 72h)
**Pressure Angle:** DIFFERENT from original
```
Original pressure: Service Quality Inconsistency
Follow-up angle: Operational Independence

"What if your branches could operate independently but report unified quality?"
```

### Follow-Up 2 (If Gate 3 fails: Page not visited after 24h)
**Pressure Angle:** SCARCITY + URGENCY
```
"We're currently supporting 5 other agencies in your market. 
Next available implementation window: [DATE]. After that: [DATE]."
```

### Follow-Up 3 (If Gate 5 fails: Conversation stalled 48h)
**Medium:** ESCALATE (not email, direct call)
```
OPERATOR CALLS:
"I know we've sent a few emails. Let me understand what you're thinking. 
What's the real objection? Budget? Timing? Feature question?"
```

### Follow-Up 4 (Still no standing order)
**Pressure Angle:** OFFER + ECONOMICS
```
"Here's exactly what this costs.
Here's what you save.
Here's our timeline.
Does that work for you?"
```

**Rule:** Never repeat same pressure type twice. Each follow-up is a different door.

## Inverse Incentive Psychology: Locked In

**The problem:** Prospects think "They're following up because they want the sale"  
**The solution:** Make each touchpoint so specific to THEIR situation that they think "They follow up because they understand my problem"

**How:**
- Email: Specific recognition (not demographic analysis)
- Page: Trust + proof (not claims)
- Operator response: Their specific question + framework (not template)
- Follow-ups: Different angles (not repetition)
- Entire journey: About solving THEIR problem (not making sale)

**Result:** Inverse incentive changes "I should ignore this" to "They actually get it"
