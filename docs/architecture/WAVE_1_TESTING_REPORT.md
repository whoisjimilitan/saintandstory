## Proof: Psychology > Templates

### Comparison: Psychology vs Hardcoded RELIEF_TEMPLATES

**Hardcoded Template (Current):**
> "One thing that stood out about ${businessName}: Customers mention ${painPointImplication || "delivery timing"}. Businesses like yours end up managing that personally. Quick question — is that your experience?"

**Issues:**
- Generic opening ("one thing stood out")
- Uses implication fallback if pain point missing
- "businesses like yours" is demographic, not observation
- No pressure-type specificity

**Psychology Engine:**
> "One thing that stood out about haart: Your best branch gets 4.8★ reviews. Your newer branch gets 3.2★. Clients consistently mention the difference. This probably sounds familiar: you're spending time managing inconsistent service quality across branches when you should be focusing on growth."

**Advantages:**
- ✅ Specific data points (actual ratings)
- ✅ Unexpected consequence (client perception)
- ✅ Operational burden named (managing inconsistency)
- ✅ Opportunity cost (should be selling)
- ✅ Pressure-type specific (Service Quality Inconsistency)

**Outcome:**
- Psychology email: Prospect feels **understood** (specific observation)
- Template email: Prospect feels **analyzed** (generic implication)
