# OPERATOR LOAD MODEL
**Cognitive load management - designing for focus, not throughput**

---

## CORE PRINCIPLE

Maximize quality, not quantity.

System prevents operator burnout by:
- Limiting daily actions
- Enforcing breaks
- Preventing decision fatigue
- Ensuring breathing room

---

## RECOMMENDED DAILY LOAD

### Total Actions Per Day

**Optimal:** 12-14 actions per day  
**Maximum safe:** 18 actions per day  
**Never show:** > 20 actions in TODAY queue  

If more actions exist:
- Overflow queued for tomorrow
- TODAY shows only top 20
- Operator sees: "14 ready, 6 queued for tomorrow"

---

### Action Breakdown (By Type)

#### New First Contacts: 4-6 per day
**Time per action:** 5-8 minutes  
**Cognitive load:** Medium (new company decision)  
**Total time:** 20-40 minutes

**Why this range:**
- Too few: Operator sees work as insufficient
- Too many: Context switching fatigue
- 4-6 maintains steady rhythm without overwhelm

**Pacing:**
- Morning (9:00-12:00): 2-3 new contacts
- Afternoon (2:00-5:00): 2-3 new contacts
- Prevents morning overwhelm

---

#### Follow-ups (7-14 day): 3-5 per day
**Time per action:** 3-5 minutes (faster, proven formula)  
**Cognitive load:** Low (follow-up email mostly pre-written)  
**Total time:** 10-25 minutes

**Why this range:**
- Most follow-ups are templates (low cognitive load)
- 3-5 keeps engagement pipelines warm
- Mix of new business + follow-ups prevents monotony

---

#### Reply Handling: 1-3 per day
**Time per action:** 5-15 minutes (varies, high engagement)  
**Cognitive load:** High (each reply is unique)  
**Total time:** 10-40 minutes

**Why this range:**
- Replies require individual attention
- Can't template-ize (each is unique)
- 1-3 replies per day is realistic average
- Prevents decision fatigue from back-to-back custom responses

**Daily variance expected:**
- Some days: 0 replies (low engagement)
- Some days: 5 replies (high engagement)
- 14-day average should be ~20 replies (1-3 per day)

---

#### Standing Order Actions: 1-2 per day
**Time per action:** 3-10 minutes (frequency adjustment, performance review)  
**Cognitive load:** Low (operational, not strategic)  
**Total time:** 5-20 minutes

**Why this range:**
- Standing orders are mostly automated
- Operator only acts on performance review or frequency change
- 1-2 per day prevents neglect without overload

---

#### Calls: Max 4 per day
**Time per action:** 10-20 minutes per call  
**Cognitive load:** HIGH (real-time conversation)  
**Total time:** 40-80 minutes

**Why this limit:**
- Calls are most draining action type
- Can't context-switch rapidly
- 4 calls = entire morning block or entire afternoon
- Maximum 2 calls back-to-back, then break required

**Pacing:**
- Call 1: 9:00-9:20
- Call 2: 10:00-10:20 (after break)
- Call 3: 1:00-1:20 (after lunch break)
- Call 4: 2:30-2:50 (after afternoon break)

## DAILY RHYTHM (Recommended Schedule)

### 9:00-10:00 AM - Morning Block 1

**Today queue view:**
1. Reply (if received): High priority
2. 2-3 new first contacts (high fit)
3. One call (if scheduled)

**Actions:** 3-5 minutes per action = 15-25 minutes  
**Result:** 3-4 completed

**Break:** 10:00-10:15 (coffee, walk, reset)

---

### 10:15-12:00 PM - Morning Block 2

**Today queue view:**
1. 1-2 follow-ups (7-day window)
2. 2-3 new first contacts
3. Standing order review (if needed)

**Actions:** 3-8 minutes per action = 20-35 minutes  
**Result:** 4-6 completed

**Lunch:** 12:00-1:00 (away from screen, full break)

---

### 1:00-2:30 PM - Afternoon Block 1

**Today queue view:**
1. One call (if needed)
2. 1-2 follow-ups
3. Standing order frequency adjustment (if needed)

**Actions:** 3-15 minutes per action = 20-40 minutes  
**Result:** 3-5 completed

**Break:** 2:30-2:45 (walk, water, transition)

---

### 2:45-4:30 PM - Afternoon Block 2

**Today queue view:**
1. 2-3 new first contacts
2. Standing order review (if not done)
3. Conversation history review (if time)

**Actions:** 3-8 minutes per action = 15-30 minutes  
**Result:** 3-4 completed

**Daily total:** 13-20 actions completed

## COGNITIVE LOAD BY ACTION TYPE

### Low Load (Can do many per day)
- Follow-up emails (template-based): ✅ 3-5 per day
- Standing order frequency adjustment: ✅ 2-3 per day
- Archive decision: ✅ 2-3 per day
- View conversation history: ✅ 4-5 per day

### Medium Load (Requires focus)
- New first contact emails: ⚠️ 4-6 per day (limit)
- Reply handling: ⚠️ 1-3 per day (limit)
- Standing order review: ⚠️ 1-2 per day (limit)

### High Load (Requires full attention)
- Phone calls: 🔴 Max 4 per day (hard limit)
- Complex decisions: 🔴 Max 2 per day (hard limit)
- Standing order creation discussion: 🔴 Max 3 per day (hard limit)

---

## DAILY LIMITS (Hard Stops)

| Action Type | Daily Limit | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Phone calls | 4 | Real-time conversation is draining |
| New decision points | 6 | Decision fatigue after 6-8 decisions |
| Complex follow-ups | 3 | Unique replies require individual focus |
| Calls > 30 minutes | 2 | Back-to-back long calls cause burnout |
| Standing order changes | 2 | Operational, not revenue-generating |

**If TODAY queue exceeds limits:**
- Excess moves to next day
- Operator sees: "10 ready today, 4 queued for tomorrow"
- Never forced to exceed

## PREVENTING DECISION FATIGUE

### Decision Fatigue Threshold

Research suggests decision fatigue sets in after:
- 6-8 unique decisions
- 2-3 hours of continuous decision-making

### System Protection

**Choice 1: Reduce unique decisions**
- Most actions are templates (low decision load)
- System provides "recommended action" (reduces to yes/no)
- Pre-drafted emails (reduces composition decision)

**Choice 2: Batch similar decisions**
- All new first contacts in one block
- All follow-ups in another block
- All reviews in another block
- Reduces context switching

**Choice 3: Limit decision windows**
- Morning block: Decisions 9:00-10:00, 10:15-12:00
- Break: 10:00-10:15 (no decisions)
- Afternoon block: Decisions 1:00-2:30, 2:45-4:30
- Breaks: Every 1.5 hours minimum

## QUALITY OVER QUANTITY

### Poor Approach (Burnout Path)
```
Goal: 30 actions per day
Result: After 20, operator is exhausted
Quality drops: Emails become generic
Outcomes: Worse response rates
Operator: Burnout in 4-6 weeks
```

### Optimal Approach (Sustainable)
```
Goal: 14 quality actions per day
Result: Operator finishes at 4:00 PM
Quality high: Personalized, thoughtful emails
Outcomes: Better response rates
Operator: Sustainable long-term (months/years)
```

**Math:**
- 14 actions/day × 250 working days = 3,500 actions/year
- 20 actions/day × 200 working days = 4,000 actions/year
- Quality difference: 40% better response rate on 14 actions = better lifetime value

Quality wins long-term.

## OPERATOR WELLNESS INDICATORS

### System Monitors

**Red flag: Today queue regularly > 18 actions**
- Suggests too many companies selected
- Or too many aged-but-not-contacted
- Or standing orders creating too much work

**Red flag: Same operator doing 5+ calls per day**
- Suggests calls not distributed
- Or call volume misestimated
- Or process needs improvement

**Red flag: Replies piling up (> 5 per day)**
- Suggests campaigns getting good response (good)
- But operator might be overwhelmed (bad)
- May need second operator or async reply batching

### System Response

**If red flags detected:**
- Notify operator: "You've handled 6 calls this week, consider distributing"
- Suggest break schedule adjustments
- Offer to queue overflow to next day
- Monitor week-to-week trends

## FLEXIBILITY IN LIMITS

### What Can Vary
✅ Morning vs afternoon split (some operators prefer later start)  
✅ Call duration preferences (some better on long calls)  
✅ New business vs follow-up mix (some better at one or other)  
✅ Break timing (some prefer longer breaks, more often)  

### What's Fixed
🔴 Daily action limit: 18 max (non-negotiable)  
🔴 Call limit: 4 per day (brain science based)  
🔴 Decision limit: 6-8 per day (decision fatigue threshold)  
🔴 Break requirement: Every 90-120 minutes (focus science)  

---

## LONG-TERM SUSTAINABILITY

### Weekly Pattern
- Monday: Medium load (warm up)
- Tuesday-Thursday: Full load (peak)
- Friday: Reduced load (wind down)
- Total: ~70 actions per week sustainable

### Monthly Pattern
- Week 1-3: Full operational load
- Week 4: Review + planning (lower operational load)
- Month 4: Light duties + training (if multi-operator team)

### Operator Tenure

**With proper load management:**
- 3-6 months: Ramp to full proficiency
- 6-24 months: Peak performance
- 24+ months: Considered expert, can mentor

**Without load management:**
- 1-2 months: Burnout signs
- 3-4 months: Performance drops
- 4-6 months: Operator leaves or requests transfer
